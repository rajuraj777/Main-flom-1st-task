Natural Environment

The natural environment is the thin layer of life and life supports, called the biosphere, that contains the earth's air, soil, water, and living organisms.

About

The natural environment encompasses all living and non-living things that occur naturally on Earth. This includes everything from plants, animals, and microorganisms to rocks, soil, water, and air. Here are some key components and aspects of the natural environment:

1. Biotic Elements: These are the living parts of the environment, such as plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms.

2. Abiotic Elements: These are the non-living parts, including sunlight, air, water, minerals, and climate.

3. Ecosystems: These are communities of living organisms interacting with their physical environment. Examples include forests, deserts, wetlands, and oceans.

4. Natural Resources: These are materials and components that can be found within the environment, such as water, minerals, and fossil fuels.

5. Natural Phenomena: These include events like earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and weather patterns.
